UniON - Child Safety Standards

Last updated: 27/11/2025

UniON is a social platform designed exclusively for university and Erasmus students aged 18 and above. Although our app is not intended for minors, we are committed to maintaining the highest safety standards and preventing any form of Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE).

This document explains our policies, prevention practices, and how we respond to reports.

1. Age Restrictions

  • UniON is not intended for users under the age of 18.
  • Creating an account as a minor is strictly prohibited.
  • We reserve the right to remove or block any account suspected of being underage.

2. Zero Tolerance Policy for CSAE

We strictly prohibit:

  • Any content depicting or suggesting child sexual abuse or exploitation.
  • Grooming behaviour or attempts to contact minors for inappropriate purposes.
  • Images, conversations, or files involving minors in sexual contexts.

Any violation will result in immediate account removal and may be reported to relevant authorities.

3. Reporting and Response Procedures

Users can report profiles, messages, events, or content they consider inappropriate or unsafe.

Upon receiving a report related to CSAE:

  • We immediately review the content.
  • We suspend the involved accounts during evaluation.
  • If necessary, we report the case to law enforcement or legally required authorities.

We aim to act on urgent safety reports within 24 hours.

4. Moderation Practices

  • User-generated content may be reviewed when flagged.
  • Reports are prioritized based on severity.
  • Multiple violations result in permanent bans.
  • We continuously update detection and moderation processes.

5. Prevention Measures

  • Age-restricted registration.
  • Profile reporting tool.
  • Block function to prevent unwanted contact.
  • Manual review of serious safety reports.
  • Strict enforcement of community guidelines.

6. Cooperation with Authorities

We fully cooperate with law enforcement agencies when cases involve:

  • Child exploitation.
  • Child abuse.
  • Endangerment or grooming activities.

We will share information strictly in accordance with applicable laws and privacy regulations.

7. Contact for Child Safety Concerns

If you need to report urgent safety concerns related to child protection, please contact:

info@unionerasmus.com
(Designated contact for safety and CSAE-related matters)

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update these standards to improve safety, compliance, or clarity. Updates will be published on this page, and the "Last updated" date will change accordingly.
