UniON - Data Deletion

Last updated: 28/11/2025

UniON is a social platform designed for university and Erasmus students aged 18 and above. We respect our users' privacy and provide a clear way to request the deletion of personal data and accounts.

This page explains how users can delete their data, what is deleted, what may remain, and how to submit a deletion request.

1. How Users Can Request Account and Data Deletion

Users can request deletion in one of the following ways:

A. In-app deletion (preferred)

2. What Data Is Deleted

When a user requests account deletion, we permanently delete or anonymize the following:

Personal Identification

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • University and Erasmus-related information
  • Profile biography
  • Gender, age, nationality
  • Interests
  • Language preferences

Profile Content

  • Profile photos
  • Uploaded images (if any)

Account Information

  • Account credentials
  • Session data
  • Device-related information

Location Data

  • City
  • Coordinates (if provided)

All personally identifiable information is removed entirely from our systems.

3. What Data May Be Retained (Anonymized)

Some data may remain in the system only after being anonymized and cannot be used to identify the former user.

This includes:

Messages in Chats

  • Chat history remains visible to other users
  • The sender will appear as ‚ÄúDeleted User‚Äù
  • No personal information is retained

Groups and Events Created by the User

  • Group or event may remain active for the benefit of other users
  • The creator will be shown as ‚ÄúDeleted User‚Äù
  • No identifying info is kept

Technical Logs

  • Some server logs may be retained temporarily for security, fraud prevention, and legal compliance
  • All logs are anonymized and cannot be linked back to a deleted account

4. Retention Period

  • Personal data is deleted immediately, or
  • Fully removed within a maximum of 30 days
  • Backups containing residual data are overwritten automatically within 90 days

This process follows GDPR and Google Play's data retention requirements.

5. Irreversibility

  • The account cannot be recovered
  • All personal data is permanently removed
  • Users must create a new account if they want to return to UniON
